With my sculptural work I sought to explore the theme of death and decay. I typically worked with plaster and metal as the two continually interact with one another. Plaster will rust metal as metal dyes plaster. Each piece is a representation of the fact that life is finite and in the end, everything dies.

Decay

This was my first attempt at using sculpture to illustrait the breakdown of eveything. The process behind this piece involved making a metal core, followed by foam and coating it in plaster. Once I had a smooth semi-sphere, I carved back into it to get the battered form displayed in the image.

Untitled

These three metal cubes started off identical. Two were coating in acid in order to wear them down. With this project I sought to display the process of erosion over time in a broader sense rather than fixate on one point in time.

Discarded

This project started off similar to my other plaster pieces, however instead of trying to wear the surface down while keeping it smooth, I took a knife and carved countless lines into the surface. The concept behind this is while everything breaks down, nothing is ever perfect to begin with. I also stopped using foam by this point as it detracted from the on going rusting effect that plaster has on metal.

Rot

With this piece I sought to improve the mutilated nature of this body of work by using charcoal to highlight the various lines I carved into it. With this piece I exposed more of the metal skeleton and experimented with getting the surface to flake in spots.
